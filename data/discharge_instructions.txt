=== GENERAL DISCHARGE GUIDELINES ===

Upon discharge from our healthcare facility, patients must follow these instructions carefully to ensure a safe and successful recovery at home.

MEDICATIONS:
- Take all prescribed medications exactly as directed by your physician.
- Do not stop taking medications without consulting your doctor first.
- If you experience side effects, contact your healthcare provider immediately.
- Keep a complete list of all medications including over-the-counter drugs and supplements.
- Medications should be stored at room temperature unless otherwise specified.
- Do not share medications with others.

FOLLOW-UP APPOINTMENTS:
- Schedule your follow-up appointment within 7 days of discharge unless otherwise instructed.
- Call our scheduling department at least 48 hours in advance to confirm appointments.
- Bring all discharge paperwork and medication list to your follow-up visit.
- If you cannot attend your appointment, call us at least 24 hours in advance to reschedule.

WOUND CARE (if applicable):
- Keep surgical sites or wounds clean and dry for the first 48 hours.
- Change dressings as instructed by your nurse.
- Watch for signs of infection: redness, swelling, increased pain, warmth, or discharge.
- Do not immerse wounds in water (bath, swimming pool) until cleared by your doctor.
- Report any separation of wound edges immediately.

ACTIVITY RESTRICTIONS:
- Do not drive for 24 hours after receiving anesthesia or sedation.
- Avoid strenuous physical activity unless cleared by your physician.
- Return to work only when cleared by your doctor.
- Light walking is encouraged to prevent blood clots, unless restricted.

DIET AND NUTRITION:
- Follow the dietary guidelines provided by your care team.
- Stay well hydrated unless on a fluid restriction.
- Avoid alcohol while taking prescription medications.
- Special diets (diabetic, low-sodium, renal) must be strictly followed.

WHEN TO SEEK EMERGENCY CARE:
Call 911 or go to the nearest emergency room if you experience:
- Chest pain or pressure
- Difficulty breathing or shortness of breath
- Sudden severe headache
- Signs of stroke: facial drooping, arm weakness, speech difficulty
- Severe bleeding that does not stop
- High fever (over 103°F / 39.4°C)
- Sudden changes in vision or consciousness
- Severe allergic reaction (hives, throat swelling)

WHEN TO CALL YOUR DOCTOR:
- Temperature over 101°F (38.3°C)
- Nausea, vomiting, or diarrhea lasting more than 24 hours
- Increasing pain not controlled by prescribed medications
- Swelling in legs or ankles
- Unusual fatigue or weakness
- Questions about your medications or care plan

CONTACT INFORMATION:
- General Inquiries: Call the main hospital line
- After-hours nurse line available 24/7
- Emergency: Always call 911

PATIENT RIGHTS:
- You have the right to receive a copy of these instructions in your preferred language.
- You may request an interpreter at no charge.
- You have the right to refuse treatment and understand the consequences.
