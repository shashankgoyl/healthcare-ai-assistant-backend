=== MEDICATION REFILL GUIDELINES ===

OVERVIEW:
This policy governs the process for requesting prescription medication refills at our facility. Patients are encouraged to plan ahead and request refills well before their medication supply runs out.

STANDARD REFILL PROCESS:
1. Submit refill requests at least 72 hours (3 business days) before your medication runs out.
2. Refill requests can be submitted via:
   - Patient portal (preferred method)
   - Pharmacy fax directly to prescriber
   - Phone call to the prescriber's office
   - Telehealth visit for eligible medications
   - In-person visit if required
3. Allow 2-3 business days for processing unless urgent.
4. Your pharmacy will be notified when the refill is approved.

ELIGIBLE MEDICATIONS FOR REFILL:
Routine refills are generally approved for:
- Chronic condition medications (blood pressure, diabetes, thyroid, cholesterol)
- Psychiatric medications (with psychiatrist or prescriber approval)
- Hormonal therapies and contraceptives
- Allergy medications
- Preventive medications

CONTROLLED SUBSTANCES:
Controlled substances (Schedule II-V) have special requirements:
- Schedule II medications (e.g., Adderall, Ritalin, opioids) CANNOT be refilled; a new prescription is required each time.
- Schedule III-V medications may be refilled up to 5 times within 6 months.
- Early refills for controlled substances are generally not permitted.
- Patients must have an active patient-provider relationship and recent in-person evaluation.
- Lost or stolen controlled substance prescriptions require a police report.
- Out-of-state transfers for controlled substances are very limited and require prescriber approval.

TELEHEALTH REFILLS:
Medication refill requests may be reviewed and approved during telehealth visits under the following conditions:
- The medication is already prescribed and the patient has an established relationship with the prescriber.
- The medication does not require a new physical examination or new lab work.
- The patient's condition is stable and well-controlled.
- Schedule II controlled substances cannot be prescribed via telehealth (in most states).
- The prescriber determines that in-person evaluation is not required.
- Telehealth platforms used must comply with DEA and state regulations for prescribing.

REFILLS REQUIRING AN OFFICE VISIT:
The following require an in-person appointment before a refill can be approved:
- New symptoms or changes in condition
- Medications requiring current lab results (e.g., warfarin, lithium, methotrexate)
- Annual or periodic monitoring required by standard of care
- First refill of certain high-risk medications
- Controlled substances requiring regular evaluation
- Any medication with safety concerns identified

VACATION/TRAVEL REFILLS:
- Advance supply of up to 90 days may be provided for travel with prior approval.
- Submit vacation refill requests at least 5 business days in advance.
- Controlled substances have strict limits on early or vacation refills.
- International travelers should consult with prescriber about local regulations.

REFILL DENIALS:
A refill may be denied for the following reasons:
- Missing required lab work or monitoring
- Overdue office visit
- Safety concerns with current dosage or drug interactions
- Controlled substance policy violations
- Expired prescription
- Insurance prior authorization issues
If your refill is denied, you will be notified of the reason and next steps.

AFTER-HOURS EMERGENCY REFILLS:
For urgent medication needs outside of business hours:
- Contact the on-call provider through the main hospital line.
- A short supply (3-7 days) may be provided to bridge until an office visit.
- This does not apply to controlled substances except in genuine emergencies.

PRESCRIPTION TRANSFERS:
- Non-controlled prescriptions can be transferred to another pharmacy of your choice.
- Controlled substance transfers are restricted and must comply with DEA regulations.
- Contact your current pharmacy to initiate a transfer.

MAIL-ORDER PHARMACY:
- Many insurance plans offer mail-order pharmacy services for 90-day supplies at reduced cost.
- Best suited for maintenance medications for chronic conditions.
- Allow 7-10 business days for initial delivery; 5 days for refills.
- Inquire with your insurance plan about mail-order benefits.

MEDICATION DISPOSAL:
- Unused or expired medications should be disposed of safely.
- Many pharmacies offer medication take-back programs.
- Do not flush medications unless the label specifically says to do so.
- Contact local DEA-authorized collection sites for controlled substances.

PATIENT RESPONSIBILITIES:
- Monitor your medication supply and request refills in a timely manner.
- Keep all scheduled appointments required for ongoing prescriptions.
- Report any side effects or changes in your condition promptly.
- Notify us of any new medications prescribed by other providers.
- Store medications as directed and keep out of reach of children.
